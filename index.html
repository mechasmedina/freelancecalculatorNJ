⚡ Freelance Tax Deductions You Can Use — Quick Reference

🏠 Home Office
$3,600/yr
IRS simplified: $5/sq ft, max 300 sq ft ($1,500). Actual method can be more. Slider shows actual method at ~10% of home costs.
💻 Equipment & Software
$3,000/yr
Laptops, monitors, cameras, subscriptions (Adobe, Zoom, etc.). Section 179 allows full deduction in year of purchase.
🏦 SEP-IRA
$15,000/yr
Up to 25% of net self-employment income, max $69,000 (2024). Reduces taxable income dollar-for-dollar. Best retirement tool for freelancers.
🏥 Health Insurance
$7,200/yr
100% of health insurance premiums for self + dependents deductible above the line. Reduces both federal and state taxable income.
🚗 Business Travel
Varies
$0.67/mi (2024 IRS rate), flights, hotels for client work. Include in "Other" slider. Keep a mileage log.
📱 Phone & Internet
~$1,200/yr
Business-use % of your phone and internet bill. 50–80% is defensible if you use them heavily for client work. Include in "Other."
📚 Professional Development
~$500–2,000/yr
Courses, certifications, books, conferences directly related to your work. Include in "Other."
⚖️ ½ SE Tax
Auto-calculated
The IRS lets you deduct half of your self-employment tax before calculating income tax. Automatically applied in this calculator.

Assumptions & Methodology

  • Corporate baseline is fixed at $95K gross, $68,101 net (NY + NYC taxes, $2,400 health premium, HoH filing, $2,000 Child Tax Credit).
  • SE Tax = 15.3% × 92.35% of gross. Half is deducted above the line before calculating federal and NJ taxable income.
  • Federal income tax brackets applied after: SE ½ deduction, standard deduction ($21,900 HoH), all business deductions, SEP-IRA, health insurance deduction. Child Tax Credit ($2,000) applied as offset.
  • NJ state tax: flat ~5.2% effective rate applied to NJ taxable income (gross minus business deductions minus SEP-IRA; NJ does not allow SE half-deduction or standard federal deduction — NJ has its own exemptions; this uses a simplified effective rate).
  • SEP-IRA cap enforced at 25% of net self-employment income (after SE tax deduction), max $69,000.
  • ACA health insurance: $7,200/yr fixed (approx Silver plan NJ, 1 adult + 1 child at this income level). Self-employed health insurance deduction applied as above-the-line federal/NJ deduction.
  • The net take-home shown is spendable cash. SEP-IRA funds are invested/deferred — not "lost." This actually gives freelancers a long-term wealth advantage the comparison does not capture.
  • No QBI (Qualified Business Income) deduction modeled — could add up to 20% deduction on net income if eligible. Consult a CPA.
  • This is an estimation tool. Consult a licensed CPA or tax professional for your actual situation.
